Module 1: Foundations of Yoga Not Started

Historical Roots

Yoga originated over 5,000 years ago in the Indus-Sarasvati civilization, with the earliest mentions in the Rig Veda. The foundational text, Patanjali's Yoga Sutras (400 CE), systematized yoga into the 8 Limbs.

Philosophical Framework

  • 8 Limbs of Yoga: Yamas, Niyamas, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi
  • 4 Paths of Yoga: Karma (action), Bhakti (devotion), Jnana (knowledge), Raja (meditation)
  • 5 Koshas: Layers of being from physical to bliss body

Modern Yoga Landscape

From Hatha Yoga (15th century) to contemporary styles like Vinyasa, Iyengar, and Yin Yoga. Scientific research confirms benefits for flexibility, stress reduction, and chronic pain management.

Module 3: Pranayama & Breathwork Locked

Breath Physiology

How breathing affects the autonomic nervous system: slow diaphragmatic breathing activates the parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) response, reducing cortisol by up to 50%.

Core Techniques

Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril)

Balances hemispheres of the brain. Technique: Close right nostril, inhale left; close left, exhale right; repeat 5-10 cycles.

Kapalabhati (Skull Shining)

Energizing breath. Technique: Passive inhales, forceful exhales (1-2 per second) for 30 seconds to 1 minute.

Box Breathing

Military technique for focus. Pattern: Inhale 4s → Hold 4s → Exhale 4s → Hold 4s.

Scientific Benefits

  • Reduces blood pressure
  • Improves HRV (Heart Rate Variability)
  • Enhances pulmonary function
  • Decreases anxiety symptoms
  • Boosts oxygen saturation

Practical Exercise

Practice 5 minutes of diaphragmatic breathing before bed for 3 nights, noting sleep quality changes.

Core Techniques

Mindfulness Meditation

Focus on present-moment awareness without judgment. Steps: 1) Anchor to breath 2) Notice distractions 3) Gently return.

Body Scan

Systematic attention through body parts. Benefits: Reduces somatic stress by 27% (Harvard study).

Mantra Meditation

Repetition of sacred sounds (Om) or phrases. Neurological effect: Increases theta brain waves.

Scientific Benefits

  • 8-week MBSR reduces amygdala density (stress center)
  • Increases gray matter in prefrontal cortex
  • Lowers inflammatory markers (IL-6)
  • Improves attention span by 16%
  • Reduces relapse in depression by 50%

Module 5: Teaching Methodology Locked

Class Structure

  1. Opening (5-10 min): Centering, intention-setting
  2. Warm-up (10-15 min): Gentle stretches, Sun Salutations
  3. Main Sequence (20-30 min): Peak poses with preparation
  4. Cool Down (10 min): Forward folds, twists
  5. Final Relaxation (5-10 min): Savasana, meditation

Cueing Techniques

Effective Cue Types

  • Anatomical: "Rotate thighs inward"
  • Energetic: "Root down to rise up"
  • Visual: "Imagine your spine like a string of pearls"
  • Breath-based: "Inhale to lengthen, exhale to deepen"
