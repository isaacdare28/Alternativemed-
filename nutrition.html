Module 1: Foundations of Nutritional Healing Not Started

Historical Perspectives

Nutritional therapy dates back to Hippocrates (460-370 BCE) who said "Let food be thy medicine." Traditional systems like Ayurveda (5000 BCE) and TCM (3000 BCE) developed sophisticated food-based healing protocols.

Core Principles

  • Bioindividuality: No one-size-fits-all diet
  • Nutrient Density: Maximizing micronutrients per calorie
  • Digestive Health: Gut as foundation of wellness
  • Detoxification: Supporting the body's elimination pathways

Modern Integrative Approaches

Combining traditional wisdom with evidence-based nutrition science. Research confirms food's role in gene expression (nutrigenomics) and microbiome balance.

Module 2: Traditional Healing Diets Locked

Ayurvedic Nutrition

Dosha-Based Eating

  • Vata: Warm, moist, grounding foods (soups, stews)
  • Pitta: Cooling, bitter foods (cucumber, coconut)
  • Kapha: Light, spicy foods (beans, bitter greens)

Traditional Chinese Medicine Nutrition

Food Energetics

  • Heating foods: Ginger, lamb, cinnamon (for cold conditions)
  • Cooling foods: Mint, cucumber, tofu (for heat conditions)
  • Dampness-reducing: Aduki beans, barley, pumpkin

Mediterranean Diet

Key Components

  • Olive oil as primary fat
  • Abundant vegetables and legumes
  • Moderate fish and dairy
  • Red wine in moderation

Module 4: Gut Health & Microbiome Locked

Gut-Brain Connection

The enteric nervous system contains 500 million neurons communicating with the brain via the vagus nerve. 90% of serotonin is produced in the gut.

Healing the Gut

4R Protocol

  1. Remove: Eliminate irritants (gluten, dairy if sensitive)
  2. Replace: Add digestive enzymes if needed
  3. Reinoculate: Probiotics and prebiotic fibers
  4. Repair: Nutrients like glutamine, zinc, omega-3s

Microbiome Superfoods

Prebiotic Foods

Garlic, onions, leeks, asparagus, bananas (green), chicory root

Probiotic Foods

Sauerkraut, kimchi, miso, tempeh, kefir, yogurt (unsweetened)

Leaky Gut Protocol

  • Bone broth: Provides collagen/gelatin
  • L-glutamine: 5g daily to repair tight junctions
  • Quercetin-rich foods: Apples, capers, onions
  • Omega-3s: Reduce intestinal inflammation

Module 5: Detoxification & Cleansing Locked

Body's Detox Pathways

  • Liver: Phase I (activation) and Phase II (conjugation)
  • Kidneys: Filter blood 60 times/day
  • Skin: Sweat eliminates heavy metals
  • Lungs: Exhale volatile compounds
  • Colon: Eliminates waste products

Foods That Support Detox

Liver Support

Cruciferous vegetables (sulforaphane), beets (betaine), dandelion greens

Heavy Metal Detox

Cilantro (mobilizes metals), chlorella (binds metals), garlic (sulfur compounds)

Safe Detox Protocols

7-Day Gentle Detox

  • Eliminate: Processed foods, alcohol, caffeine
  • Emphasize: 8-10 vegetable servings/day
  • Hydration: 2L water + herbal teas daily
  • Support: Milk thistle for liver, Epsom salt baths

Condition-Specific Protocols

  • Anti-inflammatory diet (remove gluten, dairy)
  • Omega-3 rich foods (wild salmon, flaxseeds)

Blood Sugar Imbalance

  • Low glycemic load diet
  • Cinnamon, fenugreek, bitter melon
  • Chromium-rich foods (broccoli, nuts)
  • Protein with each meal
